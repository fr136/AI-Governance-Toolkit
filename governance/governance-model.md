# AI Governance Model

## Purpose

This document defines a governance model for the controlled use of artificial intelligence within an organization.

## Governance Objectives

- Define clear accountability for AI usage.
- Prevent uncontrolled shadow AI usage.
- Protect confidential and personal data.
- Ensure human validation of AI-assisted outputs.
- Align AI usage with security, compliance and business requirements.

## Governance Principles

1. AI usage must be authorized and documented.
2. Sensitive data must not be submitted to unauthorized AI tools.
3. AI outputs must be reviewed by a qualified human.
4. AI tools must be assessed before deployment.
5. AI-related incidents must be reported and handled through a formal process.

## Governance Bodies

### Executive Sponsor

Owns strategic validation of AI adoption and accepts residual risks.

### RSSI / CISO

Defines security requirements, validates risk controls and monitors AI-related threats.

### DPO / Privacy Officer

Assesses privacy impact, personal data processing and GDPR compliance.

### Legal / Compliance

Reviews contractual, regulatory and liability exposure.

### IT Department

Controls technical deployment, access management and integration with existing systems.

### Business Owners

Define business use cases, operational requirements and acceptable use conditions.

## Minimum Governance Requirements

- AI use case registration.
- Risk assessment before production use.
- Supplier assessment for third-party AI tools.
- Human validation for critical outputs.
- User awareness and training.
- Incident reporting channel.
- Annual review of AI usage.

## Review Cycle

This governance model should be reviewed at least once per year or after major regulatory, technical or organizational changes.
